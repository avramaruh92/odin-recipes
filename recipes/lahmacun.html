Lahmacun

Description

Lahmacun, often called "Turkish pizza," is a thin and crispy flatbread topped with a flavorful mixture of ground meat, vegetables, and spices. It's a popular street food in Turkey and the Middle East, enjoyed hot and fresh from the oven. The combination of savory meat topping and soft dough makes it a delicious and satisfying meal, perfect for sharing with friends and family.

Traditionally, lahmacun is served with fresh parsley, lemon wedges, and sometimes a simple salad. You can roll it up with greens and eat it by hand, making it a fun and interactive dish for any occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup warm water
  • 1 teaspoon dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 250g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Add flour, salt, and olive oil. Knead until a smooth dough forms. Cover and let rise for 1 hour, until doubled in size.
  3. Divide dough into 4 balls. Roll each ball into a thin circle.
  4. In a separate bowl, combine ground meat, onion, tomato, bell pepper, tomato paste, garlic, parsley, paprika, cumin, salt, pepper, and olive oil. Mix well.
  5. Spread a thin layer of meat mixture evenly over each dough circle.
  6. Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C). Place lahmacun on a baking sheet.
  7. Bake for 8-10 minutes, until edges are crisp and meat is cooked.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley, lemon wedges, and optional salad.
