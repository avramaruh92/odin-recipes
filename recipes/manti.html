Manti

Description

Manti are traditional Turkish dumplings filled with spiced meat and served with a tangy yogurt sauce and melted butter. These bite-sized parcels are beloved across Central Asia and the Middle East, known for their delicate dough and flavorful filling. Making manti is a cherished family activity, often enjoyed during special gatherings.

The dumplings are boiled until tender, then topped with garlicky yogurt and a drizzle of paprika-infused butter. A sprinkle of dried mint or sumac adds a final touch, making manti a comforting and delicious dish perfect for sharing.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water (more as needed)
  • 1 egg
  • 200g ground beef or lamb
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon butter (for serving)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (for serving)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced (for yogurt sauce)
  • 1 teaspoon paprika (for butter sauce)
  • Dried mint or sumac (optional, for garnish)

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix flour and salt. Add egg and water gradually, kneading until a smooth dough forms. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Mix ground meat, onion, salt, and pepper in a separate bowl.
  3. Roll out dough thinly and cut into small squares (about 2 inches).
  4. Place a small amount of filling in the center of each square. Pinch the corners together to seal and form little dumplings.
  5. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add manti and cook for 8-10 minutes, until tender.
  6. For yogurt sauce: Mix yogurt with minced garlic and a pinch of salt.
  7. For butter sauce: Melt butter in a pan, add paprika, and stir for 1 minute.
  8. Serve manti topped with garlic yogurt, drizzle with paprika butter, and sprinkle with mint or sumac if desired.
